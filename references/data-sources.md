# Hard-Data Source Catalog

Read this when a claim has a quantitative or record-based backbone you can check against
raw data instead of reporting. Primary and near-primary sources are grouped by domain
below. The goal is the same across all of them: reach a number or record whose publisher
is accountable for it — audited, penalty-bearing, or methodologically transparent — rather
than a downstream outlet repeating it. Always confirm the dataset is current and that
you're reading the primary publisher, not an aggregator restating it.

## Markets & macro
Exchanges (price and volume direct from the venue), FRED, central-bank data portals,
TradingEconomics, SEC EDGAR and equivalent securities regulators, BIS, IMF, World Bank.

## Energy & commodities
EIA, IEA, OPEC and JODI, Platts, Argus; inventory, production, and refinery-run reports;
pipeline and storage disclosures.

## Shipping & trade flows
AIS vessel tracking (MarineTraffic, Kpler), port-authority statistics, UN Comtrade,
national customs databases, bill-of-lading aggregators.

## Corporate & legal
Company filings (10-K, 8-K, and national equivalents), official gazettes, court dockets
and PACER-like registries, regulatory enforcement actions, patent and trademark offices.

## Science & health
Peer-reviewed journals and preprint servers (note the peer-review status), WHO, CDC, ECDC,
clinical-trial registries, Google Scholar and Semantic Scholar for citation tracing,
Retraction Watch before relying on any single paper.

## Conflict, geopolitics & events
ACLED, GDELT, satellite-imagery providers, flight and ship trackers, verified eyewitness
OSINT, and Bellingcat's published methods and toolkits.

## Statistics & demographics
National statistical offices, OECD, Eurostat, census bureaus, and official labor and price
indices.
